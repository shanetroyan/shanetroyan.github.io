ABOUT ME

Hi there! My name is Shane Troyan. I am an avid tech enthusiast. I have long been fascinated with technology ever since I was just 5 years old. I was first introduced to our family's personal computer around that time. My parents purchased a PC for me soon after and I learned how to create powerpoints, play computer games such as Catz95, Hoyle Classic Board Games, and Captain Crunch. I wanted to learn more and everything I could about computers so in middle school I registered for all the computer classes that were available. When I was just 13, I learned how to port forward through the router's default gateway in order to host a Minecraft Server for friends and family to join. At 14 I built my first computer running Windows 7, operating on a intel Core i7-4770k chipset, 8GB DDR3 1600Mhz, and a 1 TB WD-Blue. I felt so accomplished after building it and my desire to learn more about electronics only grew exponentially when I got my first automobile. When I was 16 I applied at a local computer repair shop nearby and began doing basic and some more intermediate IT work. \ I created Cat5e Network cables, performed virus and malware removal, software updates, clean Windows installations, graphics card upgrades, hard drive clones, and memory upgrades to clients' pcs. Additionally, I invested in a graphics card for my now three year old PC (Nvidia GTX 960 2GB-VRAM). The next summer I managed and designed web sites for customers to improve their business sales and market exposure. I learned alot of HTML, CSS, JS, Wordpress, and MySQL Databases during that time. At 18, I decided to enroll at Oakland University to study Computer Science. After working for that initial computer repair shop I was hired at another one located a few miles away where I performed similar job duties. I managed the company's eBay page, point of sale kiosk, boxed, shipped, and labeled packages, dropped them off at the post office, and even did on-site service calls at the clients place of business. After the day of doing technical work, I would work evenings at a local fine dining establishment known as Kruse and Muer on Main in Downtown Rochester. I learned alot about customer service, speed and promptness. I worked there as a Busser, Dishwasher, and Food Runner. I enjoyed being able to socialize with the waiters, and other patrons. When I was 20, I attended the IT and Computer Science Career Fair and received multiple interviews at top fortune 500 companies. I received an award offer for an internship position from Ally Financial. The Summer of 2020 I completed my internship and met a lot of wonderful people.
